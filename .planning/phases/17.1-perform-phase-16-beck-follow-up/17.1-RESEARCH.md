## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Editing only two of the four consistency surfaces
**What goes wrong:** flipping the principle-backing row + Sources block but leaving the intro
blockquote saying the material is no-oracle -> internal contradiction.
**How to avoid:** treat surfaces A+B+C (+D) as one atomic edit per upgraded row; the blockquote is
easy to miss.

### Pitfall 2: Citing the book as the owned source
**What goes wrong:** tagging a row `Owned; oracle-verified` while its Source cell still names
"Test-Driven Development by Example" (a `Access: book` summary-only entry) -> violates D-05; the tag
is dishonest and un-gateable.
**How to avoid:** the owned Source cell must name the FREE essay actually gated (Canon TDD / First
One, Then Many).

### Pitfall 3: Rewriting recommendation content
**What goes wrong:** the executor "improves" the distilled rationale prose while re-attributing ->
scope creep; risks a no-verbatim or fidelity failure.
**How to avoid:** content is expected already-correct (well-known Beck TDD); touch attribution/tier
only. A fidelity revise comes ONLY from the gate, as an own-words directive.

### Pitfall 4: Forcing a candidate/conditional row
**What goes wrong:** upgrading degenerate-case / assert-first / evident-data without a gate PASS ->
a dishonest owned tag on a surface the free essay does not actually cover.
**How to avoid:** the gate decides #4/#5/#6 (D-03/D-04/D-05). "Blocked" -> revert to no-oracle.

### Pitfall 5: Reading `.oracle/` essay prose in main context
**What goes wrong:** breaks the DST-04 clean-room; copyright + provenance integrity.
**How to avoid:** author reads only `.oracle/*/index.md`; only the orchestrator-driven agent reads
essay bodies, and only own-words verdicts cross back.
